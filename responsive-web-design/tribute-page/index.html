Sir Tim Berners-Lee

The inventor of the World Wide Web, the HTML markup language, the URL system, and HTTP

  • June 8, 1955: Born in London to parents who were engineers of the world's first commercially built computer.
  • 1973: Graduated from Queen's College, Oxford, with a first-class degree in Physics.
  • 1980: Worked as a freelance consultant at CERN from June to December, where he proposed a project based on hypertext to facilitate sharing information among researchers.
  • March 5, 1989: Wrote the first proposal for the World Wide Web, describing it as a "large hypertext database with typed links."
  • October 1990: Began work on the World Wide Web, developing the first web browser and server.
  • August 6, 1991: Launched the first website, providing information about the World Wide Web project.
  • October 1994: Established the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) at MIT to develop web standards.
  • 2004: Became a Professor of Computer Science at the University of Southampton, focusing on the Semantic Web.
  • July 16, 2004: Knighted by Queen Elizabeth II for his pioneering work on the World Wide Web.
  • November 2009: Launched the World Wide Web Foundation to advance the web as a medium for positive change.
  • May 2012: Became President of the Open Data Institute, promoting the use of open data.
  • October 2013: Launched the Alliance for Affordable Internet, aiming to provide affordable internet access globally.
